Understanding Stuttering

A comprehensive guide to stuttering, its causes, types, and management techniques

What is Stuttering?

Stuttering is a complex communication disorder affecting speech fluency.

Click to learn more about stuttering

Stuttering affects the fluency of speech, where the flow of words is interrupted by:

  • Involuntary repetitions
  • Prolongations of sounds
  • Silent pauses or blocks

The Causes of Stuttering

Explore the factors behind stuttering
  • Genetic predisposition
  • Neurological factors
  • Environmental influences
  • Developmental delays

Types of Stuttering

Word Repetition

Repeating whole words or phrases (e.g., "I-I-I want")

Common in children
Sound Repetition

Repeating individual sounds (e.g., "S-s-sunday")

Needs attention
Prolongation

Extending sounds (e.g., "Ssssssunday")

Common type
Blocks

Temporary inability to produce sounds

Most challenging
Interjections

Adding extra sounds (um, uh, like)

Mild form
Circumlocution

Word substitution to avoid difficult sounds

Coping mechanism
