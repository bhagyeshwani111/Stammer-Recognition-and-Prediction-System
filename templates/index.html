About The Project

The "Stammer Recognition and Prediction System Using AI" is an innovative solution that helps identify and support disfluencies in speech such as stammering, mumbling, fumbling over words, and abrupt interruptions.

Unlike common speech recognition systems, this project focuses specifically on stammering behaviors, including repetition of sounds, elongation of syllables, and unexpected stops. It utilizes advanced speech-to-text models like Google's Speech-to-Text and Mozilla DeepSpeech to process audio into text.

The processed text undergoes analysis through NLP models such as n-grams and LSTM networks, which have been fine-tuned to predict the next word or phrase a user might speak, considering the flow of disfluency within the context of their speech.

The system aims to improve the quality of life for those with speech disorders, enabling them to participate with greater confidence in social, professional, and personal interactions.
